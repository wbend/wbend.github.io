About these charts

This report contains the results of the SIT Study Abroad Long-Term Impacts and Outcomes survey, a research project designed to investigate how alumni would self-report impacts of their study abroad experience with regard to academic, personal, and professional outcomes. The paper focuses largely on the career outcomes of the alumni who participated in this research. The findings indicate that a large percentage of SIT Study Abroad alumni report that impacts to their career choices were significant and that their study abroad experience was helpful in some unique ways when applying for graduate school or employment opportunities. The results of the survey allowed for exploration of what specific fields alumni have gravitated towards, and to some extent, how they were influenced by their study abroad experience to choose these fields. Results seem to indicate that SIT Study Abroad alumni are highly accomplished and socially conscious, choosing the education, health, and nonprofit fields in large margin over other fields.
